OLIVE OIL

Olive oil is a liquid fat that comes from olives which is a tree crop.The olives are first picked and washed and processed right after harvest. Rollers are used to crush the olives into a paste. The paste then mixes water into the paste slowly to allow the oil molecules to group together. This mixture is stirred for 20 to 40 minutes . This is done in closed mixing chambers to prevent exposing the oil to the air which would significantly reduce its quality. This mixture is then heated to about 82 degrees Fahrenheit. Afterwards, this paste is placed on mats and pressed again or sent through a centrifuge where the water and oil are extracted from the oil. The solid that remains is called pomace in which steam or other solvents are used to squeeze whatever oil is left. Moving forward, the oil may be refined, bleached, and/or deodorized depending on the manufacturing plant.
