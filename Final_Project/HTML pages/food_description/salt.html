SALT

Sea salt is naturally derived from the evaporation of shallow waters from the sun and wind. Similarly, the sea salt that we eat today is produced by placing seawater in large concentrating ponds to allow evaporation from the sun and wind. This is often done in dry climates because evaporation can only be achieved in low rain falls. Rock salt can be found underneath the Earth’s surface due to ancient underground waterways that have long dried up. They’re extracted through dynamite and then crushed for industrial and purposes besides food. This type of salt differs from sea salt in which it has many minerals and other impurities. Mined salt can also be refined into the production of table salt. It’s dissolved in water then purified through precipitation, and this process repeats, giving us iodized salt.
