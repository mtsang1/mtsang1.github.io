HONEY

Honey starts off as a flower nectar which is collected by bees. This gets broken down into simple sugars that are stored inside the honeycombs. Due to the design of the honeycomb and the fanning of the bee’s wing, evaporation causes evaporation and creates this sweet liquid honey. Interestingly, the color of the honey depends on the type of nectar the bee collected from.

Beekeepers then harvest the honey by scraping off the wax cap off each cell that bees use to seel off the honey. After removing these caps, the frame is then placed in a centrifuge that spins the frames and forces honey out of the comb.

After the honey is harvested, the honey is then strained to remove any remaining wax and other particles. The honey is then bottled up into a container which is the type of honey we would see at the farmer’s market without any extra ingredients added into the honey.
