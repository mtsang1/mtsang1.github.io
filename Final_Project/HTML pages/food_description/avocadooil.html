AVOCADO OIL

The high oil content from the avocado has been known since the Aztec times. The flesh of the avocado can contain up to 30% oil and has originally been extracted for cosmetic use. The avocado is harvested first when itâ€™s mature but not overripe. Avocado oil is pressed from the pulp surrounding the pit of the fruit of the avocado by a mechanical extraction similar to the olive oil extraction. The flesh is first removed from the seed and skin. This is then grounded to a paste and then mixed slowly for 40-60 minutes at a slightly higher temperature than olive oil . The oil and water are then separating using a decanting centrifuge. The pulp and seed are returned to be used for further planting of animal feed.
