MAPLE SYRUP

Maple syrup comes from tapping maple trees (sugar maple, red maple, or black maple trees). This involves drilling a hole into the tree where the spout is tapped. When the temperature rises, the sap flows through this spout which goes through a tubing system to the collection tanks. The sap is then taken to large storage tanks where they are evaporated where the water is boiled off. When the liquid reaches 107F degrees, it has become maple syrup. After evaporation, it goes through a filtering process and is packaged.
