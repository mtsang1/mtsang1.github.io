MINT

Today, the United States produces 70% of the world’s mint as it's predominantly seen in holidays like Christmas. Mint doesn’t produce seeds, but rather new fields are planted from existing plants. When the plants are mature enough, they are mowed and left dried for 24 to 36 hours. They are then picked up and blown into tubs, Oil can be extracted from the leaves using steam distillation to create mint oil.
