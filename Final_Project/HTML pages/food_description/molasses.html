MOLASSES

Most common forms of molasses come from sugar cane or sugar beet. Manufacturers crush them in order to extract the juice. This is boiled down to a syrup which then sugar crystals are extracted from the syrup. The remaining substance is molasses. Everytime manufacturers repeat this process, a different type of molasses is produced. Light molasses is produced after the first boiling and has the sweetest taste. Dark molasses is produced after the second boiling, producing a thicker, less sweet taste. Blackstrap molasses is produced after the third boiling and has the thickest and bitter taste.
