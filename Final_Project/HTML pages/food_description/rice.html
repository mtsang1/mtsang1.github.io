RICE

Rice was believed to have first been domesticated in the Yangtze River valley in China and is the staple food for most of the world’s population. It is a seed that comes from the grass species Oryza glaberrima and is led by China and India’s production. The first step to harvesting rice is draining the rice paddy. Farmers then cut the plants by machine or hand and laid out to dry for two or three days. Next, the rice is separated from the stalk which can also be done by hand or machine. One common method for separation is banging the plants against the table which will carry the grains that fall through it. The rice is then dried again (because it contains so much water from the paddies) and milled using a rice huller in order to remove the chaff, which are the outer husks of the grain. Brown rice, on the other hand, isn’t milled which is why it’s thicker than white rice.
